The origin chapter

Before the founder story, there was nightlife.

After moving to San Diego in 2006, William entered Hillcrest nightlife and began learning to DJ after an invitation from DJ Nikno. What started behind the booth grew into years of club sets, Pride-related bookings, promotional experiments and community-centered nightlife work. The schedule eventually extended beyond San Diego to Splash in San Jose and Executive Suite in Long Beach.

The DJ years taught him how rooms feel, how audiences move and how trust is built in real time. Those lessons later became part of the foundation for Hillcrest Social, Out at the Fair and William’s broader work creating spaces where people feel seen.

Digital innovation

The brand extended beyond the booth.

Recorded mixes and podcast episodes streamed directly to phones, while DJ WillZ-Mobile gave clubgoers event information, photos and a live request form. It was an early example of a local nightlife brand building its own digital ecosystem—later recognized in William’s 2016 San Diego Metro 40 Under 40 profile.

The legacy

The booth was training for everything that came next.

DJ Will Z was not a disconnected past life. The nightclub years trained William to understand audiences, create energy, market experiences and build belonging in public. The tools changed—from turntables to fair stages, books and microphones—but the purpose stayed remarkably consistent.
