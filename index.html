01 / The full picture

Not a reinvention.
A reveal.

For years, people knew William primarily as the founder behind Out at the Fair—an LGBTQ+ community program built inside one of America’s most traditional public spaces.

But the real skill was never simply producing events. It was reading a room, building trust, packaging an idea, bringing partners together, creating visibility and turning a complicated mission into an experience people could actually feel.

That same instinct now powers a broader ecosystem: books inspired by a real-life throuple, a weekly video podcast, a creator brand, speaking, consulting and a public story about what happens when your life becomes bigger than the thing you were once known for.

Community architect

Belonging in unexpected places

William’s signature work has been creating LGBTQ+ visibility where audiences were not necessarily expecting to find it—and making the experience feel welcoming to everyone.

Founder in motion

Building beyond one flagship idea

The next chapter is not about abandoning OutAt. It is about placing it inside a larger body of work built around story, connection and cultural impact.

Real-life storyteller

The public brand is the life

The relationship, the work, the books, the humor, the pivots and the unfinished future are no longer separate projects. Together, they are the platform.
